Module 02

How tickets arrive

Three routes — all land in the same Help Desk.

📧 Email to Inbox
Teacher emails the CSA support address
HubSpot automatically creates a ticket from the email
The email thread lives on the ticket record
Replies from HubSpot go back to the teacher
🌐 Website Form
Teacher submits a support form on educate.ie
Form submission creates a ticket automatically
Ticket type pre-filled based on form selection
Teacher details pulled from their contact record
✋ Manual Creation
You or a Sales rep creates the ticket directly
Used when a teacher calls in or contacts Sales
Create from the Help Desk or from a contact record
Always link to the correct contact and school
Module 02
Ticket stages at educate.ie
Every ticket moves through these stages from open to resolved.
→
New — just arrived, not yet assigned
→
In Progress — assigned to a CSA, being worked
→
Waiting on Teacher — you’ve responded, awaiting their reply
→
Escalated — passed to Sales or another team
→
Closed — resolved and completed
Module 02

Internal notes vs replies

Getting this right protects teachers from seeing internal team comments.

🔒 Internal Note
Visible to your team only — never sent to the teacher
Use for: flagging to a colleague, adding context, @mentioning Sales
Appears in the ticket timeline with a yellow background
Always use for internal communication on a ticket
📤 Reply
Goes directly to the teacher’s email
Use for: answering the query, requesting more info, confirming resolution
Appears in the ticket timeline and in the teacher’s inbox
Double-check before sending — this goes to the teacher
✅ Closing a Ticket
Only close when the issue is fully resolved
Set the ticket type before closing (see Module 3)
Add a closing note with outcome
Closed tickets still appear in the teacher’s history

Module 03

Common ticket types at educate.ie

Always set the ticket type before closing. This is how we report on what’s coming in.

📚 eBook Access
Teacher can’t access a digital resource
Check: is their school’s licence active?
Check: are they using the correct login?
Most common type — usually resolved quickly
📦 Missing Sample
Teacher expected a sample that hasn’t arrived
Check the associated Sales task — was it dispatched?
Check delivery address on the school record
May need Sales rep involvement if sample not yet sent
🔐 Login Issue
Teacher can’t log in to a platform
Check which platform (EdPal, digital textbook, etc.)
Reset process varies by platform — check the knowledge base
Log the platform name and error in the ticket
📋 Book List Query
Question about a book list or adoption
Often needs Sales involvement — flag early
Check the school’s book list status on the company record
May be time-sensitive — escalate if adoption deadline near
Module 03

Setting priority — what goes first

🔴 High Priority
Teacher can’t access materials needed for a class today or tomorrow. Adoption deadline at risk. Complaint escalating. Anything affecting a school’s ability to teach.
🟡 Medium Priority
Missing sample where teacher is still deciding. Login issue that isn’t blocking a class. Query about book list with no immediate deadline. Standard support questions.
🟢 Low Priority
General information requests. Future-dated queries. Questions that don’t require immediate action. Anything that can wait 2–3 working days without impact.
⚡ Triage Rule
Spend 5 minutes at the start of every day reviewing new tickets. Set type and priority before you start working. A triage first means nothing urgent slips through.
Module Summary
Module 3: Ticket Types & Triage

03

04
Use filters in Help Desk to see tickets by type, priority, and age. Never let a ticket go stale.

Reading a school record
Everything the team has logged about this school in one timeline.
→
Timeline (centre panel) — every call, note, email, task, and ticket
→
Filter by activity type — see only notes, or only tasks, or only tickets
→
Associated contacts (right panel) — all teachers at this school
→
Open tickets (right panel) — any unresolved issues linked to this school
Module 04

What to look for before you respond

📅 Recent Sales Activity
Has a rep visited recently? Was a sample promised? Is there a deal in progress? This tells you what commitments have been made to this school.
🎫 Existing Tickets
Is there already an open ticket for this teacher or school? If yes, update that ticket — don’t create a duplicate. Check before creating anything new.
📝 Previous CSA Notes
Has this issue come up before? Has another CSA already dealt with this teacher? The history tells you what was said and what was resolved.
🏫 School Stage
Is this school actively using our books? Are they new or lapsed? A school considering adoption needs a different response than one that’s been with us for years.

Module 05

When to escalate vs when to resolve yourself

↑

Escalate to Sales

✔ Teacher is asking about a book adoption decision
✔ School is unhappy and at risk of not renewing
✔ Query relates to pricing, deals, or proposals
✔ A sample was promised but never dispatched
✔ Teacher has escalated a complaint beyond support
✓

Resolve yourself

✔ eBook login or access issues
✔ Sample not arrived (if already dispatched)
✔ Platform or account queries
✔ Delivery address updates
✔ General product or resource questions
Module 05

How to hand off a ticket to Sales

A clean handoff means Sales has everything they need before they pick it up.

Step 1 — Add an Internal Note
Summarise the issue clearly
Include what you’ve already done
@mention the relevant Sales rep
Note any urgency or deadlines
Step 2 — Update the Ticket Stage
Move the ticket to “Escalated”
Re-assign the owner to the Sales rep
Keep the ticket open until Sales confirms it’s resolved
Don’t close a ticket you’ve escalated — Sales closes it
Step 3 — Create a Task if Needed
If action is needed (call the school, send a quote), create a task
Assign to the Sales rep with a due date
Link the task to both the ticket and the school record
This keeps it in their task queue — nothing gets lost
Module Summary
Module 5: Escalations & Handoffs
01
Escalate when the query involves adoption, renewals, complaints, pricing, or undelivered promises.
02
Add an internal note with context, @mention the Sales rep, and move the stage to “Escalated”.
03
Re-assign the ticket to the Sales rep. Don’t close it — they close it when resolved.
04
Create a task for Sales if a specific action is needed. Link it to the ticket and the school.

Module 06

At-risk signals to watch for

These don’t always mean a school is lost — but they always mean a school needs attention.

⏰ No Activity
No calls, notes, or visits logged in 60+ days
No open tasks for this school
Last contact was before an issue was raised
School has gone quiet after a complaint
🎫 Ticket Patterns
Multiple unresolved tickets from the same school
Same issue reported more than once
Tickets that have been open longer than your SLA
Negative or frustrated tone in teacher replies
📉 Relationship Signals
Teacher contacts you directly to complain about Sales
School has asked to be removed from mailing lists
Renewal period is approaching with no engagement
Account health property set to “At Risk” by Sales
Module 06
Account Health property
Set on the Company record. Visible to both CSA and Sales.
→
Healthy — school is active, engaged, issues resolved
→
Needs Attention — something to watch, not urgent yet
→
At Risk — escalate to Sales immediately, flag in internal note
→
Update this property when you spot a signal — don’t wait for Sales to notice
Module Summary

03

04
CSAs often see signals before Sales does — your early flag can save a school relationship.

Sales leads the renewal conversation — but the quality of CSA support is what makes it possible.

Your notes give Sales the full picture before their call

Module 08
Your daily HubSpot routine
A consistent routine means nothing slips through and your queue stays clean.
1
🌅 Start of day: Check Tasks Due Today. Check Open Tickets — Mine. Triage anything new: set type and priority.
2
🎫 Work your queue: High priority first. Update every ticket you touch — stage, notes, and owner.
3
📝 Log everything: Every call, reply, and action gets logged on the record. Same day, every time.
4
🔚 End of day: No unassigned tickets. No overdue tasks. Flag anything you couldn’t close with a note and a follow-up date.
A clean queue at end of day = a calm start the next morning.
